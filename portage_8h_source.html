30 /*
31  @namespace Portage
32  The Portage namespace houses all of the code within Portage.
33 
34  Cells (aka zones/elements) are the highest dimension entities in a mesh
35  Nodes (aka vertices) are lowest dimension entities in a mesh
36  Faces in a 3D mesh are 2D entities, in a 2D mesh are 1D entities
37  BOUNDARY_FACE is a special type of entity that is need so that process
38  kernels can define composite vectors (see src/data_structures) on
39  exterior boundary faces of the mesh only
40 
41  Wedges are special subcell entities that are a simplicial
42  decomposition of cell. In 3D, a wedge is tetrahedron formed by one
43  point of the edge, the midpoint of the edge, the "center" of the
44  face and the "center" of the cell volume. In 2D, a wedge is a
45  triangle formed by an end-point of the edge, the mid-point of the
46  edge and the center of the cell. In 1D, wedges are lines, that are
47  formed by the endpoint of the cell and the midpoint of the
48  cell. There are two wedges associated with an edge of cell face in
49  3D.
50 
51  Corners are also subcell entities that are associated uniquely with
52  a node of a cell. Each corner is the union of all the wedges incident
53  upon that node in the cell
54 
55  Facets are the boundary entity between two wedges in adjacent
56  cells. In 3D, a facet is a triangular subface of the cell face
57  shared by two wedges in adjacent cells. In 2D, a facet is half of
58  an edge that is shared by two wedges in adjacent cells
59  */
