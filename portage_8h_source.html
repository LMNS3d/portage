67 /*
68  @namespace Portage
69  The Portage namespace houses all of the code within Portage.
70 
71  Cells (aka zones/elements) are the highest dimension entities in a mesh
72  Nodes (aka vertices) are lowest dimension entities in a mesh
73  Faces in a 3D mesh are 2D entities, in a 2D mesh are 1D entities
74  BOUNDARY_FACE is a special type of entity that is need so that process
75  kernels can define composite vectors (see src/data_structures) on
76  exterior boundary faces of the mesh only
77 
78  Wedges are special subcell entities that are a simplicial
79  decomposition of cell. In 3D, a wedge is tetrahedron formed by one
80  point of the edge, the midpoint of the edge, the "center" of the
81  face and the "center" of the cell volume. In 2D, a wedge is a
82  triangle formed by an end-point of the edge, the mid-point of the
83  edge and the center of the cell. In 1D, wedges are lines, that are
84  formed by the endpoint of the cell and the midpoint of the
85  cell. There are two wedges associated with an edge of cell face in
86  3D.
87 
88  Corners are also subcell entities that are associated uniquely with
89  a node of a cell. Each corner is the union of all the wedges incident
90  upon that node in the cell
91 
92  Facets are the boundary entity between two wedges in adjacent
93  cells. In 3D, a facet is a triangular subface of the cell face
94  shared by two wedges in adjacent cells. In 2D, a facet is half of
95  an edge that is shared by two wedges in adjacent cells
96  */
