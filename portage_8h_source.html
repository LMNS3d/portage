41 /*
42  @namespace Portage
43  The Portage namespace houses all of the code within Portage.
44 
45  Cells (aka zones/elements) are the highest dimension entities in a mesh
46  Nodes (aka vertices) are lowest dimension entities in a mesh
47  Faces in a 3D mesh are 2D entities, in a 2D mesh are 1D entities
48  BOUNDARY_FACE is a special type of entity that is need so that process
49  kernels can define composite vectors (see src/data_structures) on
50  exterior boundary faces of the mesh only
51 
52  Wedges are special subcell entities that are a simplicial
53  decomposition of cell. In 3D, a wedge is tetrahedron formed by one
54  point of the edge, the midpoint of the edge, the "center" of the
55  face and the "center" of the cell volume. In 2D, a wedge is a
56  triangle formed by an end-point of the edge, the mid-point of the
57  edge and the center of the cell. In 1D, wedges are lines, that are
58  formed by the endpoint of the cell and the midpoint of the
59  cell. There are two wedges associated with an edge of cell face in
60  3D.
61 
62  Corners are also subcell entities that are associated uniquely with
63  a node of a cell. Each corner is the union of all the wedges incident
64  upon that node in the cell
65 
66  Facets are the boundary entity between two wedges in adjacent
67  cells. In 3D, a facet is a triangular subface of the cell face
68  shared by two wedges in adjacent cells. In 2D, a facet is half of
69  an edge that is shared by two wedges in adjacent cells
70  */
