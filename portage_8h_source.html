40 /*
41  @namespace Portage
42  The Portage namespace houses all of the code within Portage.
43 
44  Cells (aka zones/elements) are the highest dimension entities in a mesh
45  Nodes (aka vertices) are lowest dimension entities in a mesh
46  Faces in a 3D mesh are 2D entities, in a 2D mesh are 1D entities
47  BOUNDARY_FACE is a special type of entity that is need so that process
48  kernels can define composite vectors (see src/data_structures) on
49  exterior boundary faces of the mesh only
50 
51  Wedges are special subcell entities that are a simplicial
52  decomposition of cell. In 3D, a wedge is tetrahedron formed by one
53  point of the edge, the midpoint of the edge, the "center" of the
54  face and the "center" of the cell volume. In 2D, a wedge is a
55  triangle formed by an end-point of the edge, the mid-point of the
56  edge and the center of the cell. In 1D, wedges are lines, that are
57  formed by the endpoint of the cell and the midpoint of the
58  cell. There are two wedges associated with an edge of cell face in
59  3D.
60 
61  Corners are also subcell entities that are associated uniquely with
62  a node of a cell. Each corner is the union of all the wedges incident
63  upon that node in the cell
64 
65  Facets are the boundary entity between two wedges in adjacent
66  cells. In 3D, a facet is a triangular subface of the cell face
67  shared by two wedges in adjacent cells. In 2D, a facet is half of
68  an edge that is shared by two wedges in adjacent cells
69  */
